About the Author

Jerry Emeseh is a dedicated Christian author and minister passionate about spreading the Gospel and igniting spiritual revival. With a deep commitment to biblical truth, Jerry's writings focus on calling believers back to God's ways, addressing moral decline, and promoting global evangelization. As the founder of Jesus Andreious Army, Jerry leads efforts to equip the church for impactful ministry. His books and tracts are inspired by divine messages, aiming to transform lives and strengthen faith. Based in Abuja, Nigeria, Jerry continues to serve through teaching, writing, and partnership initiatives.

Our Collection

Jesus Message and Warning to American Churches and Ministers

This book delivers a divine message from Jesus Christ to Americans, their churches, and ministers, highlighting the moral decline in a nation once called “God’s own country.” Despite technological advancements and the motto “In God We Trust,” society has turned away from God, leading to its current state. Jesus expresses deep concern for the faithful remnants who uphold truth and righteousness. It serves as a warning and call to repentance for churches, pastors, and believers worldwide to return to God’s ways. The teachings aim to benefit present and future generations until Christ's return.

Igniting the Fire of God's Revival Across Europe

This book stresses that revival is a divine awakening for spiritually dead or lukewarm believers, akin to the Laodicean church in Revelation 3. Revival rekindles God’s fire and zeal, distinct from mere religious activity. While sinners need salvation, backslidden believers require revival to restore passion for God. It originates from the Holy Spirit, bringing transformation, earthly impact, and heavenly joy. Without revival, the church remains ineffective; with it, spiritual life and power are renewed.

Jesus and the Apostles Model for Global Evangelization

This book critiques how many Christians prioritize personal blessings over Jesus' mission of world evangelization. Few dedicate themselves to saving the lost, instead seeking God for selfish needs. It urges readers to join the rare few who share God’s heart for spreading the gospel. Such individuals please God, as exemplified by Timothy in the Bible. The focus is on serving Christ’s kingdom through global outreach.

Maximizing Your Thought Process and Acting to Attain Success and Remaining Successful

This book provides a practical guide to achieving all-round success in spirit, soul, and body. True success encompasses more than wealth or fame; it's about balanced fulfillment. Many succeed in one area but fail in others, resulting in incomplete success. By applying its principles, readers can grow spiritually, maintain emotional and mental health, and care for physical well-being. It offers actionable steps for total success and a purposeful life.

Is Following Jesus and Christianity Become a Joke in This Generation?

As Jesus entered Jerusalem amid cheers of “Hosanna,” the crowd hailed him as king, expecting earthly rule. The disciples shared this excitement, but Jesus wept over the city's blindness to peace and salvation. He foresaw Jerusalem's destruction due to rejecting him as Messiah. This moment illustrates that while people seek external change, Jesus brings spiritual transformation. His tears reflect deep love and sorrow when humanity misses his true purpose.

The Truth of the Gospel as It Relates to the Following Subjects in Scriptures for Believers in Christ

This book addresses misunderstood biblical subjects like worldliness, legalism, faith and works, sin, redemption, perfection, grace, eternal security, tithing, first fruits, offerings, prosperity gospel, divorce, remarriage, polygamy, Paul's epistles versus Christ's teachings, and women's ordination as pastors. Guided by the Holy Spirit, it presents accurate scriptural perspectives. These topics are crucial for strengthening believers' walk toward eternity. Readers should approach prayerfully, allowing the Holy Spirit to enlighten them. The goal is to eliminate confusion, confirm sound doctrine, and foster a fruitful Christian life.
